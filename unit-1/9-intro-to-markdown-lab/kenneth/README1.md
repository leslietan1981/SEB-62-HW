# **How to build the perfect hamburger**

![the perfect hamgurger](https://cdn.apartmenttherapy.info/image/upload/f_jpg,q_auto:eco,c_fill,g_auto,w_1500,ar_4:3/tk%2Fphoto%2F2025%2F06-2025%2F2025-06-burger-doneness-guide%2Fburger-doneness-guide-0195)

## **Building the perfect hamburger requires 80/20 ground chuck, minimal handling, and high-heat cooking. Create 1/3 lb to 1/2 lb patties with a center dimple to prevent bulging, seasoning only just before cooking. Sear on a hot grill or cast iron, add cheese, and assemble on a toasted bun with fresh toppings to prevent sogginess.**

### **_The Patty (The Core)_**

- Meat Ratio: Use ground chuck with an 80/20 lean-to-fat ratio for maximum juiciness.
- Handling: Keep meat cold and handle gently to prevent a tough, dense burger.
- Shape: Form patties about 3/4-inch thick and 1 inch wider than the bun to allow for shrinkage.
- The Secret Technique: Press a small dimple (indentation) into the center of the patty with your thumb to stop it from puffing into a ball.
- Seasoning: Season generously with salt and pepper only just before cooking to prevent the salt from drying out the meat.

### **_Cooking (The Technique)_**

- Heat: Ensure your grill or cast-iron skillet is very hot.
- Flip: Sear for 3-5 minutes, then flip once. Do not press down on the patty with a spatula, as this releases juices.
- Cheese: Add cheddar, gouda, or classic American slices in the final 30 seconds of cooking, allowing them to melt properly.

### **_The Assembly (The Structure)_**

- Bun: Use brioche for sweetness, pretzel for durability, or potato buns. Toast them with butter or fat to create a moisture barrier.\*\*
- Layering (Bottom to Top):

1. Bottom Bun
2. Sauce (e.g., aioli, mayo, or special burger sauce)
3. Lettuce (Iceberg for crispness)
4. Tomato and Pickles
5. Patty with Melted Cheese
6. Top Bun

> ### Pro Tips

- Rest: Let the cooked patties rest for a few minutes.
- Binder: Add crushed crackers or Panko breadcrumbs for a classic, juicy texture.
- Vegetables: Add caramelized onions, fried mushrooms, or a fried egg for extra flavor.
